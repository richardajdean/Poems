RICHARD ARTHUR JOHN DEAN

HAIKU FROM A CORNISH FISHING VILLAGE

The fish is landed

A purple line of blood meets

Clear blue green water


Only the wind rings

The bell now ropes snap and shout

The old man the boats


Honeysuckle scent

Between here and the next house

They placed milestones


On the beach a man

Blesses the sea and gives thanks

Gulls are wind riding


Running off the cliff

Boys jump through the air to the

Sea floating in both


When the wind blows here

The sand is not buffeted

But carried gently


A dead seal becomes

A piece of leather slowly

Small waves suck at stones


After the spring tide

Seaweed draped across the sand

And across the rocks


Calling and calling

But the sea never hears them

An unbroken wave


An empty rockpool

Waits to be filled with empty

Water and empty
